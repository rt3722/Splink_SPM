üîç Why BlockingPy?

Traditional deterministic blocking (exact matches on fields) misses records with:

Issue Traditional BlockingPy
Typos ("John" vs "Jhon") Missed Caught
Spelling ("Smith" vs "Smyth") Missed Caught
Missing Fields Missed Caught
Format Variations Partial Caught
HNSW Algorithm
Hierarchical Navigable Small World graphs provide O(log n) lookup time with high recall. Used by Statistics Poland for official statistics.

üìä Why Splink?

Splink implements the Fellegi-Sunter probabilistic model, providing:

  • Mathematical Foundation
    Bayes factors quantify evidence for/against matches
  • Unsupervised Learning
    EM algorithm estimates parameters without labeled data
  • Rich Comparisons
    Fuzzy matching, array intersection, numeric thresholds
  • Scalability
    DuckDB backend handles millions of records
# Our blocking strategy blocking_rules = [ block_on("block"), # BlockingPy ANN block_on("firstname_cleaned", "lastname_cleaned"),# Exact block_on("firstname_soundex", "lastname_soundex"),# Phonetic ]

Higher Recall

Production Ready

Both libraries are battle-tested. Splink by UK Ministry of Justice; BlockingPy by Statistics Poland for official population statistics.

üìå Conclusion

The Splink + BlockingPy combination provides the optimal balance of recall (catching all duplicates including those with data quality issues) and precision (avoiding false merges through probabilistic scoring). This hybrid approach significantly outperforms either method alone, making it the ideal choice for SPM's entity resolution needs.
